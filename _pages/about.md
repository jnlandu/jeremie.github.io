Welcome! I am Jeremie Nlandu-Mabiala, a data scientist and a graduate student in machine learning at the African Masters in Machine Intelligence (AMMI) in Senegeal, Mbour. 


I earned a Honours in Mathematical Sciences from the [Universite de Kinshasa](https://www.unikin.ac.cd) where I volunteered for two years at the Department of Mathematics, Statistics, and Computer Sciences. I also hold a MSc in Mathematical Sciences from [the Stellenbosch University](https.www.sun.ac.za) and the [African Institute for Mathematical Sciences](https:www.aims.ac.za). My academic work has been on [Functional Data analysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Functional_data_analysis) and [Gaussian Process](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Processus_gaussien) under supervision of Professor [Dabo](https://sites.google.com/view/sophie-dabo-niang/) from [Universite de Lille](https://www.univ-lille.fr/). My honours' work that can be found [here] layed on the so-called [Geometry of Banach Spaces](https://www.sciencedirect.com/handbook/handbook-of-the-geometry-of-banach-spaces/vol/1/suppl/C) and the [Theory of Operators](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operator_theory) in Functional Analysis. Preceeded by a short essay on Topological Spaces, where I studied the Metrizability of the Compact-open Topology and discussed its Separation properties.
